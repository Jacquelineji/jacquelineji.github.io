My takeaways

It is fun working in a space that involves both digital interfaces and the physical world. It also feels very impactful to work on features used by many people around me.

What I've learned the most at Uber is to take into consideration of the different real-life scenarios and tailor the solution accordingly. Because our product involves the physical space, factors such as road conditions, location accuracy, the confidence level of our prediction can have a huge impact on the experience we are designing. Working at Uber challenge me to always think about the edge cases, accessibility issues, and what could go wrong.

Another key takeaway for me is the importance of data-driven design. It came as a shock to me to learn through our previous XPs that sometimes a color change could cause a significant change in the metrics. I learned the importance of making hypotheses based on real data and collaborating closely with the PMs and UXRs to verify those assumptions.
